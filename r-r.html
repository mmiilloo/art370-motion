    HOW TO EXPORT FROM AFTER EFFECTS

    1. select the composition you wish to export. In this case, it’s named ‘final’


    2. navigate to File > Export > Add to Adobe Media Encoder Queue...
      You can export directly from AfterEffects, however using the Media Encoder is less-taxing on your computer and also allows for more options.


    3. under ‘format’ select H.264 from the dropdown menu. This is a video codec that is compatible with streaming and is less bulky than .mov files.


    4. lots of other options exisit. H.264 is most-suitable for our streaming video needs.


    5. if you click on the blue H.264 text, a new window will pop up with additional options. Here, you can use presets (Vimeo 1080p Full HD in this case). This particular video is 1920px wide by 1080px high; a standard HD video size and ratio (16:9). However, not all videos have to be 1920×1080! They can be any dimension or ratio you need; portrait orientation, square, ultra-wide, etc. Here is where you'd change that to ensure the output matches your intent.


    6. check the range of the export. You can set the start and end points of the output here (this can be helpful to render a preview)


    7. other export options that might need tweeking: audio, pixel aspect, performance... On this panel, you can adjust the output that best suits your needs (for example, you don't need audio for a silent video which would lower the file size). Certain platforms might have different file size, aspect ratio, or codec requirements.
